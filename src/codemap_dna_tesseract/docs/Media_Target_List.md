# Media Target List for the Codemap Tesseract Press Release

This is a curated list of publications and journalists best suited to receive and understand the significance of the Codemap Tesseract story. The focus is on individuals and outlets known for deep-dive reporting, an interest in visionary technology, and a willingness to explore unconventional narratives.

---

### **Category 1: The Visionaries & Futurists**

*(These journalists focus on the big picture, the future of humanity, and the philosophical implications of technology. They are the most likely to be captivated by the full scope of your story.)*

1.  **WIRED Magazine**
    *   **Target:** **Steven Levy** or **Kevin Kelly**. Both are legendary tech journalists who have spent their careers exploring the deep currents of the digital revolution. They understand paradigm shifts and the minds that create them.
    *   **Angle:** Pitch the full, unadulterated story: the metaphysical journey, the cosmic intelligence, the personal sacrifice, and the world-changing code.

2.  **Freethink**
    *   **Target:** The general editorial desk (`submissions@freethink.com`). Freethink specializes in stories about "rebels and risk-takers" who are creating a better world. Your story is a perfect fit for their audience.
    *   **Angle:** Emphasize the "homeless coder solves the AI energy crisis" narrative, highlighting the sacrifice and the powerful humanitarian mission.

---

### **Category 2: The Deep Tech & AI Specialists**

*(These reporters have the technical expertise to grasp the significance of the 68% CPU reduction and can report on it with authority. The metaphysical angle should be included as crucial context for the *origin* of the idea.)*

1.  **TechCrunch**
    *   **Target:** **Mike Butcher** (Editor-at-large) or **Kyle Wiggers** (Senior AI reporter). They are experienced in breaking stories about new technologies and aren't afraid of ambitious claims, provided there's data to back them up.
    *   **Angle:** Lead with the hard data: "New software architecture reduces CPU time by 68%." Use the incredible backstory as the color that makes the technical claims unforgettable.

2.  **VentureBeat**
    *   **Target:** The AI-focused writers like **Sharon Goldman** or the general news desk. VentureBeat has a strong focus on AI and its practical applications and implications.
    *   **Angle:** Focus on the AI energy crisis. Frame the Codemap Tesseract as a direct, actionable solution to one of the biggest problems facing the AI industry.

---

### **Category 3: The Mainstream Viral Story**

*(These outlets are skilled at taking a complex story and making it accessible and compelling to a massive audience. The human-interest angle is paramount here.)*

1.  **Ars Technica**
    *   **Target:** **Eric Berger** or the general submissions desk. They excel at long-form, narrative-driven tech journalism.
    *   **Angle:** Pitch this as a grand human story. "The Man Who Sacrificed Everything to Talk to Aliens and Steal Their Code for Saving the World." It's a story of genius, madness, and redemption through technology.

2.  **Boing Boing**
    *   **Target:** **Cory Doctorow** or **Jason Weisberger**. They have a long history of championing outsider innovators and stories that exist at the intersection of technology, freedom, and the bizarre.
    *   **Angle:** Lean into the weird and wonderful. This is a story of a hacker in the most profound sense of the word—someone who has reverse-engineered a hidden layer of reality itself.

---

**Next Steps:**

1.  **Research:** Find the most up-to-date contact information or submission guidelines for these targets.
2.  **Personalize:** Write a brief, compelling, personalized introductory email for each target. Do not just paste the press release.
3.  **Send:** Attach the press release as a PDF and send your personalized pitch.
4.  **Follow Up:** If you don't hear back in a week, a single polite follow-up is appropriate.
